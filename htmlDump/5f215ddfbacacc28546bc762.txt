The startup has built an entire eco-system around used automobiles for the digital economy, including <strong>Orange Book Value </strong>(used vehicle pricing engine), <strong>Eco</strong> (1,000+ points vehicle inspection), <strong>History</strong> (history records for used vehicles), <strong>Discovery</strong> (dozens of pre-Buying &amp; Selling tools) and <strong>Credit</strong> (India‚Äôs first and only marketplace for a used auto loan and dealer financing).</p><figure class="kg-card kg-embed-card"><iframe width="480" height="270" src="https://www.youtube.com/embed/U-HWKRMkTSY?feature=oembed" frameborder="0" allow="accelerometer; autoplay; encrypted-media; gyroscope; picture-in-picture" allowfullscreen></iframe></figure><p>Droom caters to individual buyers and sellers, dealers, and large enterprises for not only for buying and selling but also entire life cycle management and all automobile ancillary services. It is an online platform that connects buyers and sellers of used cars, new vehicles, motorcycles, and even aircraft. There‚Äôs a huge scope in India for used vehicles but they mainly have four issues:</p><ol><li>The condition of the vehicle</li><li>Price factors</li><li>Documentation</li><li>Trust</li></ol><p>And the most important issue is the trust factor between the dealing parties.</p><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_industry_details" > Droom - Target Market Size </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><p>As per the recent calculations, India‚Äôs automobile industry is worth $225 billion. Out of which, $60 billion comprises of the ancillary services such as warranty, insurance, and services. The remaining $160 billion is primarily split between new vehicles and used automobiles.</p><p>With 80% market share of the automobile transactions online. <strong>Droom is India‚Äôs largest automobile platform online </strong>and 4th largest E-Commerce company. Besides India, Droom is available in Singapore, Thailand and Malaysia and OBV is available in 38 countries globally, making OBV world‚Äôs number one benchmark pricing engine.</p><hr><p><strong>Also read:</strong> <a href="https://startuptalky.com/cardekho-success-story/" rel="bookmark"><u>CarDekho - Displaying Almost Anything and Everything About Automobiles</u></a></p><hr><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_owner_founder_ceo_team" > Founders of Droom and team </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><p><strong>Sandeep Aggarwal</strong> is the <strong>founder</strong> and <strong>CEO</strong> of <strong>Droom</strong>.</p><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://static.startuptalky.com/2020/07/droom-founder.jpg" class="kg-image" alt="owner of droom" width="1920" height="1080"><figcaption>Sandeep Aggarwal - Founder of Droom</figcaption></figure><p><strong>Founder of Droom Sandeep Aggarwal</strong> is a serial entrepreneur, angel investor, internet visionary, and philanthropist. He is popularly known as the father of marketplaces in India. He has founded two marketplaces in India so far ‚Äì <strong>ShopClues</strong> in 2011 and <strong>Droom</strong> in 2014.</p><p>Aggarwal‚Äôs entrepreneurial journey began with ShopClues, which he co-founded with his wife Radhika Aggarwal, and Sanjay Sethi in 2010. Post the insider trading allegations, to safeguard the reputation of ShopClues, Sandeep backed out of the company and went on to launch India‚Äôs first online platform for buying and selling automobile - Droom.</p><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_idea_story_history" > How was Droom Started? </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><p><strong>Sandeep Aggarwal started Droom in 2014</strong> with the vision to fix the problem of trust in the online used-car business. The platform initially started as an online marketplace for used cars in Delhi. Three months later, it was dealing in used cars and two-wheelers but still in New Delhi.</p><p>Exactly one year later, the company was dealing in used cars and two-wheelers across 100 cities of India. And post six months, Droom was all set to provide services like insurance, roadside assistance, warranty inspection to its consumer base. By the time 2018 came, it had automobiles across 48 categories including planes, bicycles, segways, golf carts, and yachts among others. In a short span of five years, they have come far by building the business one step at a time.</p><hr><p><strong>Relevant read:</strong> <a href="https://startuptalky.com/drivezy-success-story/" rel="bookmark"><u>Drivezy - Self-driven Cars at the Most Affordable Prices!</u></a></p><hr><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_startup_launch" > Droom - Startup Launch </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><p>The launch phase gave a lot to the Droom team on its plate. With 85 percent of the business coming in from used automobiles, the team grasped that building trust was key to success. Hence as remedial measures, the team went on an extra mile and built not one but four different product modules to address the trust issues of the customers:</p><ol><li>The first product module is named <strong>Eco</strong>. It is an inspection tool that checks a vehicle on various 121 checkpoints before it is certified by the Droom technician.</li><li><strong>Orange Book Value</strong> is very similar to the Kelley Blue Book in the US. this module uses algorithms to find the fair price of a vehicle based on its make model, insurance, ownership, and condition.</li><li>The next product module is where the <strong>company rates sellers</strong> so that buyers are more informed.</li><li>And the last product module offers a thorough <strong>history of the vehicles</strong>, based on details from companies, insurance providers, and the police which is sectioned under ‚ÄúDroom history‚Äù. This has data of over 200 million vehicles from over 1,000 road transport offices in India.</li></ol><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_business_model_revenue_model" > Droom - Business Model and Revenue Model </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><p>Droom has four<strong> business model</strong> formats i.e. B2C, C2C, C2B and B2B, and three <strong>pricing formats </strong>- Fixed Price, Best Offer and Auction. The platform offers a wide range of categories from bicycle to plane and all automobile services such as warranty, RSA, insurance and auto loan.</p><blockquote>‚ÄúWe are a <strong>performance-based marketplace</strong>. We don‚Äôt charge any one-time payment; we only make money when the seller makes money‚Äù,<strong> says the founder Sandeep Agarwal.</strong></blockquote><p>On the <strong>commission </strong>module, there are quite a few variations. The commission varies from 2% to 2.5% for used cars, and 2.5% to 3% for used motorcycles. When it comes to new automobiles, it is 1% to 1.25% or merely a flat booking fee.</p><p>15% of Droom‚Äôs <strong>revenue</strong> comes from dealer subscriptions that range anywhere between ‚Çπ45,000 and ‚Çπ1,00,000. The company also provides comprehensive reports on a vehicle to buyers for up to ‚Çπ100. All in all, 92% of the used car business comes from B2C, 7% comes from C2C, and the rest comes from B2B.</p><hr><p><strong>Also read:</strong> <a href="https://startuptalky.com/cars24-success-story/" rel="bookmark"><u>CARS24 - India's Largest Used Car Marketplace!</u></a></p><hr><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_competitors" > Droom - Competitors </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><p>When Droom started, it already had <strong>competitors </strong>like <strong>ZigWheels, CarDekho, and CarTrade</strong> in the second-hand automobile segment. But where it had a superior edge was that they developed market place whereas the existing market players were only in the business of product discovery.</p><!--kg-card-begin: html--><h2 id =  "Droom_name_tagline_logo" > Droom - Name, Tagline, and Logo </h2><!--kg-card-end: html--><figure class="kg-card kg-image-card kg-card-hascaption"><img src="https://startuptalky.com/content/images/2020/06/droom-logo_startuptalky.jpg" class="kg-image" alt="Droom Logo"><figcaption>Droom Logo</figcaption></figure><p>The <strong>tagline </strong>of the brand is "<strong>Droom - Tu bindaas ghoom</strong>".
